To all the teachers piloting a brand-new, virtual classroom, you got this! Whether you are leaping into virtual teaching based on your own decision or as a response to the pandemic, supporting children through remote learning can be more effective than using traditional classroom methods. For starters, online courses give students full command over their learning. They can work at their own speed, moving faster through areas of the course they feel comfortable with and slower through the ones they need a little more time on. Retention rates with online learning are better too. According to a study by the Research Institute of America, e-learning boosts retention by 25% to 60%.
